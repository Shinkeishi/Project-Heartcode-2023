HARASSMENT

Harassment covers a wide range of behaviors of offensive nature. It is commonly understood as behavior that demeans, humiliates, and intimidates a person


What is online harassment? Harassment in the context of cyberbullying refers to the use of electronic communication, such as the internet and social media platforms, to repeatedly intimidate, threaten, or belittle individuals. Cyberbullying can take various forms, including sending hurtful messages, spreading rumors, sharing private information, creating fake profiles, or engaging in any other online behavior with the intent to harm someone emotionally or psychologically.

  • spreading rumours, ridiculing, and/or demeaning others
  • harassing others because of their race, religion, sexual orientation, disability or transgender identity seeking revenge or deliberately embarrassing a person online
  • engaging in unwanted behaviour of a sexual nature via text, email or other electronic and/or social media including using intimate images or recordings of another person impersonating others, even through their own accounts, with malicious intent
  • sending inflammatory, inappropriate, rude and/or violent messages or comments about someone to provoke responses from other users exposing others to unwanted or offensive content in digital groups or meetings
